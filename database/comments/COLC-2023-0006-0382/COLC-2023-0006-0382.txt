Copyright must stringently be defended as the sole right of human creators capable of independent thought and original composition. Artificial intelligence and algorithmically produced materials are fundamentally derivative, an amalgamation of previously copyrighted materials, and cannot be considered original works. The livelihoods and societal valuation of art and artists, be they authors, writers, actors, musicians, or any others, are directly threatened by AI-produced works and the false impression that the product of complex computation based on stolen elements of copyrighted material in any way contains a &quot;spark of creativity&quot;
