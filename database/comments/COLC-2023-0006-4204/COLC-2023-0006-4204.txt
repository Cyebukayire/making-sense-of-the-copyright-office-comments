Copyright should be reserved solely for human-made works, and labors of consciousness. Until technology companies can prove that their computers and tools are capable of human-like consciousness, their infinite amounts of output or derived works shouldn&#39;t be awarded any claims of copyright.<br/><br/>AI models have been trained by works derived from human beings, some copyrighted materials, but mostly text, images and data &quot;scrapped&quot; from the internet. AI works are therefor a derivative from human labor and should be placed in the public domain, free to be used by any individual as they see fit. <br/><br/>Works generated from AI can and should be watermarked and contain metadata from the source, including its model number and any data sets used to train the generated the works.
